Understanding Diabetes



Diabetes is a complex condition that can impact your health in several ways. It's crucial to grasp the potential causes behind it to better protect yourself. Diabetes is a chronic disease that disrupts how your body processes food into energy. Most of the food you consume is broken down into sugar, also known as glucose, and released into your bloodstream.

Potential Causes of Diabetes



There are various factors that can contribute to the development of diabetes, including:


  • Genetics: A family history of diabetes can increase your risk.
  • Lifestyle: Unhealthy eating habits and a lack of physical activity can play a significant role.
  • Weight: Being overweight or obese is a common risk factor when it comes to diabetes .
  • Age: Risk increases with age, particularly after 45.
  • Other Health Conditions: Conditions like high blood pressure or polycystic ovary syndrome can increase your risk.

Importance of Getting Checked



Recognizing the signs and understanding your risk is the first step toward better health. If you suspect you might be at risk or if you're experiencing symptoms like increased thirst, frequent urination, or unexplained weight loss, it's essential to get tested for diabetes.
